Our Heritage

A Banquet Born
from Royalty

Wazwan is Kashmir's most revered culinary tradition — a royal feast of up to 36 courses, each dish a testament to centuries of Mughal-influenced, Persian-kissed Kashmiri craftsmanship. The word itself tells the story: waz means cook, wan means shop.

Every dish is prepared according to halal traditions using slow-fire techniques passed down through generations. Guests are seated in groups of four, sharing from the large copper traem, beginning with the ritual hand-washing of the Tash-t-naer.

At Kashmiri Wazwan, we honour this ancient feast — bringing the full ceremonial experience of a Kashmiri wedding banquet to every table, every day.

The Wazwan

Signature Dishes

🍖
Rogan Josh
روغن جوش

Slow-cooked lamb in a rich, aromatic gravy of Kashmiri red chilli, dried ginger, and whole spices. The crown jewel of any wazwan feast.

🥩
Rista
رستہ

Delicate hand-pounded mutton meatballs simmered in a vibrant red fennel-spiced gravy — a dish that demands patience and mastery.

🍗
Tabak Maaz
ٹابک ماز

Ribs of lamb, first simmered until tender then shallow-fried to a golden crisp. Fragrant with milk, cardamom, and saffron.

🍲
Gushtaba
گشتابہ

The grand finale — giant mutton balls cooked in a velvety yoghurt curry. Traditionally the last savoury course of the royal banquet.

🥘
Yakhni
یخنی

Lamb slow-braised in whisked yoghurt with fennel, ginger powder, and cardamom. Subtle, silky, and deeply comforting.

🌶️
Seekh Kabab
سیخ کباب

Seasoned minced mutton threaded on skewers and grilled over charcoal — the traditional opener of every wazwan, served with fragrant rice.

The Ritual

How a Wazwan
Unfolds

I
Tash-t-naer

Guests begin with a ritual hand-washing using a portable copper basin carried by attendants — a gesture of welcome and purification.

II
The Traem Arrives

A large copper platter heaped with saffron rice, quartered by two seekh kababs, arrives for four guests to share as one.

III
The Courses Flow

Course by course, the waza brings Rista, Rogan Josh, Tabak Maaz, Yakhni — each dish telling a chapter of Kashmir's culinary story.

IV
Gushtaba & Kehwa

The feast closes with Gushtaba — the grand finale — followed by fragrant Kashmiri Kehwa tea with saffron, cinnamon and crushed almonds.
